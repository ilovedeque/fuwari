## Prompt

(CNN 2016 - 2017) **Write a paragraph of 150 words: Do you agree or disagree with the statement: "He who asks a question remains a fool for five minutes; he who does not ask a question remains a fool forever". State your opinion and give reasons and examples to support your answer.**

## Notation

$(n)$: The $n^{th}$ paragraph.

## Hưng

$(1)$ In this day and age, as education is becoming an inseparable part of every individual's life, there lies the saying above. From my perspective, I agree with this statement because it bears a rather wise meaning, as well as emphasising the importance of curiosity and the courage to request help. In my interpretation, this saying means that when an individual needs assistance in learning something, they will have to expose their lack of knowledge, thus being labelled as "a fool". However, since the individual has now absorbed this new knowledge, they will only "remain a fool for five minutes". On the other hand, when a person does not reveal that assistance is needed, they will never be able to grasp the solution, and as a result, they "remain a fool forever". Therefore, when individuals have the courage to ask questions, they will understand the exposed material better than those who stay quiet, and moreover, have the curiosity to dive deeper. For example, when solving difficult math problems, one will get closer to the solutions when they request additional assistance than those who only work with the provided material. In conclusion, this saying encourages people to ask for help and not stay silent whenever they are stuck with a task.

## Vân

$(1)$ True knowledge does not begin with knowing everything but with questioning what we do not know. Therefore, I strongly agree with the statement "He who asks a question remains a fool for five minutes; he who does not ask a question remains a fool forever". Nowadays, many people feel embarrassed when they find something unfamiliar. Instead of asking questions, they choose to remain silent for fear that others will think they are unintelligent. However, the fact is that staying silent does not make you become smarter, it only leads to continued misunderstanding. For instance, a student might feel embarrassed when they ask the teacher to explain a concept they still do not understand, but the explanation will help them to stop repeating the mistakes they have made, while a student who pretends to understand just because they are afraid of being judged will gradually fall behind. Moreover, why should people be afraid of being judged when they are confused? No one is perfect. Everyone makes mistakes and gets confused, but that is how we learn and improve ourselves. Therefore, we should not feel embarrassed simply because we are unfamiliar with new concepts, instead, being willing to ask questions is the key to success.
