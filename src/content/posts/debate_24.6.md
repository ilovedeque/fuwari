**Translated (English):** THR viewing the outcome of lifting the ban on same-sex marriage in 2014 as a success for organizations promoting gender equality in Vietnam.

### My Preparation (Argument 2):

**Point:** Consider the outcome a success $\implies$ feminist organizations gain motivation to maintain + pursue further action to contribute to gender equality.

**Explanation:**

$(1)$ In status quo, in order for feminist orgs to be considered "successful" $\implies$ they must be known by supporters + the larger society to have had "good contributions" $\iff$ they must have taken observable action that has good, observable impacts for their stakeholder (in this case it is LGBTQ+ people).

$(2)$ When the ban on same-sex marriage is lifted $\iff$ lives of LGBTQ+ people are directly affected, society knows about this change + organizations must have taken substansive action on this cause for it to have taken place $\implies$ proven that this serves as real good impacts for these organisations $\implies$ considered as a success.

$(3)$ $\implies$ Organisations are widely recognised, society changed how they look at LGBTQ+ ppl (proven in Arg 1) $\implies$ potentially gain more supporters $\implies$ can get more ppl to advocate for gender equality $\implies$ maintain + pursue further action to contribute to gender equality.

$(4)$ This act does not mean that equality has been met, it just means that the organisations have taken a big successful step in going for equality.

**Impacts:**

$(1)$ Creates a base for these organisations to run campaigns (due to gaining more supporters, funding and media attraction)

$(2)$ Change society in its ways of perceiving activism (considering success $\implies$ gain more traction that it works $\implies$ more ppl willing to advocate for equality $\implies$ better in pursuing equality)
